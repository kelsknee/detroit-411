Welcome to Detroit

Everything you need to know about living in the city trash days, parking rules, city services, and more. Links go directly to official city sources so the info stays current.

🗑️ Trash & Recycling
Find Your Pickup Day
Trash is picked up every week. Your day depends on your address use the city's tool to look it up. You can also text your street address to (313) 800-7905 to sign up for weekly trash & recycling reminders.
🔍 Find my pickup day 📱 Text for reminders
Recycling Opt-In Program
Detroit's recycling program is opt-in you must sign up to receive a blue cart. Once enrolled, recycling is picked up every week on the same day as trash. Accepted: bottles, cans, cardboard, paper, and plastics. No plastic bags, no food waste, no Styrofoam.
♻️ Sign up for recycling
Bulk & Yard Waste Pickup
Leaves, branches, and grass clippings go out seasonally (spring through fall) not in your regular trash cart. For large items like furniture and appliances, Detroit offers a paid bulk pickup service or free citizen drop centers.
🍂 Yard waste info 📦 Paid bulk pickup 🆓 Free drop centers
Missed Pickup or Illegal Dumping
If your trash wasn't picked up or someone dumped illegally near your home, report it online through the Improve Detroit portal or call Public Works. The city aims to respond within 2 business days.
📱 Report via Improve Detroit 📞 (313) 224-7000
🚗 Parking
Street Cleaning No Parking Days
Most Detroit streets have designated street cleaning days posted on signs. Typically one side of the street is cleaned in the morning, the other in the afternoon. Check the signs on your block they'll show day, time, and which months it applies.
🧹 Street maintenance info
Snow & Ice Winter Parking Rules
During snow and ice events, move your car off major streets so plows can clear them. Violations result in ticketing. Sign up for Detroit Alerts 365 to get emergency notifications by text or email including snow events, boil water advisories, and severe weather.
❄️ Snow & ice management 🔔 Sign up for Detroit Alerts 365
Pay Parking & Meters
Detroit uses pay-by-plate parking via the ParkDetroit app or kiosks. Rate: $1/hour, Mon–Sat 7am–10pm. No payment needed evenings (10pm–7am), Sundays, or on major holidays (New Year's Day, MLK Day, Good Friday, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Veterans Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, New Year's Eve). Max 2 hours 7am–6pm; up to 4 hours after 6pm.
📱 ParkDetroit app
Residential Parking Permits
Some Detroit neighborhoods have residential permit zones you'll need a permit to park on your block during restricted hours. Permits are managed through ParkDetroit. Check the city's news page for current residential parking zones and enforcement schedules.
🏠 Get a permit via ParkDetroit 📋 Residential zone info
Pay a Parking Ticket
Pay parking tickets online through the Municipal Parking Department. You can also pay in person at the parking office. Have your ticket number ready.
💳 Pay a parking ticket
💧 Water & Utilities
Start or Transfer Water Service
New to Detroit? Set up your water account with Detroit Water & Sewerage Department (DWSD) within 10 days of moving in. You'll need your address, ID, and deposit. Do it online, by phone, or in person at 735 Randolph St.
💧 Start water service 📞 (313) 267-8000
Pay Your Water Bill
Pay online through the DWSD customer portal, by phone, by mail, or at authorized payment locations across Detroit. Bills are issued every two months. Set up autopay to avoid late fees.
💳 Pay online 📞 (313) 267-8000
Water Assistance Programs
Struggling to pay your water bill? DWSD offers several assistance programs including WRAP (Water Residential Assistance Program) for bill reduction and shutoff protection for low-income households.
🤝 Water assistance programs 📞 (313) 267-8000
DTE Energy Electric & Gas
Most Detroit residents get electricity and natural gas through DTE Energy. Start, stop, or transfer service online. Give DTE your move-in date in advance to avoid gaps. Assistance programs available for low-income households.
⚡ Start/stop DTE service 📞 (800) 477-9000
🗳️ Voting & Civic Life
Register to Vote
Michigan allows same-day voter registration at the polls. Register online up to 15 days before an election, or in person at your local clerk's office or at the polls on Election Day with proof of residency. Sign up for election reminders by SMS or email.
✅ Register online 🗳️ Detroit Elections 🔔 Election SMS/email alerts
Find Your Polling Place
Your polling place is assigned by address. Find yours on the Michigan Voter Information Center. Detroit also has early voting at multiple locations before Election Day check the city clerk's site for dates.
📍 Find my polling place 📬 Absentee ballot info
Find Your City Council Rep
Detroit has 7 district council members plus 2 at-large members. Your district rep is your direct contact for neighborhood issues, city services, and local concerns. Use the district map to find your rep.
🗺️ District map 🏛️ City Council
Mayor's Office & City Services
The Mayor's Action Center connects residents to city departments, tracks service requests, and handles complaints. You can also use the Improve Detroit app to report issues and track resolution in real time.
🏙️ Mayor's Office 📱 Improve Detroit
🏫 Schools & Education
Enroll in Detroit Public Schools
Detroit Public Schools Community District (DPSCD) is the main K–12 public school system. Enrollment is open year-round. You'll need proof of residency, immunization records, and prior school records. Call or visit the enrollment office.
📚 DPSCD enrollment 📞 (313) 873-6897
Find a School by Address
Use the DPSCD school locator to see which schools serve your address, including your assigned neighborhood school and schools of choice options. Detroit also has many public charter schools with open enrollment.
🗺️ School locator 🏫 Charter school info
Head Start & Free Preschool
Free early education programs are available for income-eligible children across Detroit. Use the Great Start to Quality finder to locate free preschool and Head Start programs near you. Apply early spots fill quickly.
👶 Find free preschool near me
Adult Education & GED
Want to earn a GED or high school completion diploma? Detroit at Work connects you to free adult education programs across the city, including GED prep, ESL classes, and job skills training.
🎓 Adult education & GED
🪪 ID, Licenses & Vehicle Registration
Michigan Driver's License or State ID
New Michigan residents must get a Michigan license within 30 days. Visit a Secretary of State branch Detroit's closest is at 3046 W Grand Blvd. Bring your out-of-state license, proof of residency (two documents), and Social Security number.
🪪 Get a MI license or ID 📍 Find SOS branch
New to Michigan?
If you're new to Michigan, the Secretary of State has a guide covering everything you need to do getting a license, registering your vehicle, and updating your records. New residents have 30 days to transfer their license and register their vehicle.
🙋 New resident guide
Register or Transfer a Vehicle
Register your vehicle within 30 days of establishing Michigan residency. You'll need your title, proof of insurance, and payment for registration tabs. Michigan requires no-fault auto insurance make sure you're covered before you drive.
🚗 Register/transfer vehicle 🔄 Renew tabs & plates
Update Your Address
Moved within Michigan? Update your address with the Secretary of State within 10 days. You can do it online for free no branch visit needed. Also update your voter registration at the same time.
📬 Update address online
📋 Permits, Property & Taxes
Building Permits
Doing home improvements? Many projects require a building permit from BSEED including additions, electrical work, plumbing, HVAC, decks, and fences. Unpermitted work can create legal and insurance problems. Check what's required before you start.
🔨 Building permits 📋 Permits & plan review 📞 BSEED (313) 224-2733
Property Taxes
Pay Detroit property taxes online through PayDetroit365. Summer taxes are due July 31; winter taxes are due February 14. Homeowners who live in their home may qualify for the Principal Residence Exemption (PRE), which reduces your taxable value.
💳 Pay property taxes 📄 PRE exemption info 📥 PRE form (PDF)
Property Tax Assistance
Struggling with property taxes? The City of Detroit offers assistance programs to help homeowners avoid foreclosure and reduce their tax burden. Contact the Office of the Assessor to learn what you may qualify for.
🤝 Property tax assistance 🏡 UCHC housing help
🐾 Pets & Animals
Dog Licenses
All dogs in Detroit must be licensed annually through the City of Detroit. You'll need proof of rabies vaccination. Fees are lower for spayed/neutered dogs. Unlicensed dogs can result in fines.
🐕 License your dog
Animal Control & Reporting
Report stray, injured, dangerous, or neglected animals to Detroit Animal Care & Control. Submit a report online via the city's form or visit their page for more info. For loose dogs creating an immediate safety threat, call non-emergency police.
🐾 Detroit Animal Care & Control 📝 Report an animal online 📞 (313) 224-1426
🚨 Safety & Emergency
Emergency vs. Non-Emergency
911 is for life-threatening emergencies only. For non-emergency police matters noise complaints, minor incidents, vandalism, or general concerns call (313) 267-4600. Using 911 for non-emergencies slows response for real ones.
🆘 911 (emergencies) 📞 Non-emergency police
Know Your Police Precinct
Detroit has 12 police precincts. Knowing yours helps you connect with your local officers and attend community policing meetings. Use the NPO finder to find your Neighborhood Police Officer by address.
🚔 Precincts & NPOs 📍 Find my NPO
Emergency Alerts
Sign up for Detroit Alerts 365 to get text and email alerts about snow events, boil water advisories, severe weather, and city emergencies. Takes under a minute to sign up.
🔔 Sign up for Detroit Alerts 365
Warming Centers

During cold weather months, the City of Detroit operates warming centers for homeless residents. During extreme cold (10°F or below), daytime hours are extended. All sites provide two hot meals, showers, counseling, and sleeping accommodations.

Fire Safety & Prevention
The Detroit Fire Department offers free smoke detector installation for Detroit residents. Call to request they'll come to your home. Smoke detectors save lives; Detroit law requires working detectors on every floor.
🔥 Detroit Fire Dept 📞 (313) 224-4800
🌳 Neighborhood Life
Join or Start a Block Club
Block clubs are one of the best ways to meet neighbors, address local issues, and improve your block. Detroit has hundreds of active block clubs. The Department of Neighborhoods runs the official Block Club Program contact them to find yours or get support starting one.
🏘️ Block Club Program
Detroit Parks & Recreation
Detroit has 300+ parks and a citywide recreation system with pools, community centers, and sports programs. Many programs are free or low-cost for Detroit residents. Search programs and register online.
⛳ Parks & Recreation 🏊 Recreation programs 📅 Find your fun & register
Detroit Public Library Card
A Detroit Public Library card is free for all Detroit residents. It unlocks 22 branches, free Wi-Fi, e-books, digital magazines, museum passes, tutoring, and job resources. Sign up in person at any branch with a photo ID and proof of address.
📚 Get a library card
Detroit ID Card
The Detroit ID Card is a free, city-issued photo ID available to all Detroit residents regardless of immigration status or housing situation. Issued through the Detroit Health Department. Useful for residents who don't have a state ID or driver's license.
🪪 Detroit ID Card program
Report Issues & City Services
For general city service questions potholes, streetlight outages, fallen trees, graffiti use the Improve Detroit portal. You can track your request and see what else has been reported on your block.
📱 Improve Detroit 📋 City department directory
Free Tax Preparation
Detroit residents can get free tax prep at city-sponsored VITA sites. They also help you claim the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) worth thousands for qualifying families. Find a site near you on the city's tax prep page.
💵 Free tax prep sites 💰 EITC info
