Welcome to Detroit

Everything you need to know about living in the city — trash days, parking rules, city services, and more. Links go directly to official city sources so the info stays current.

🗑️ Trash & Recycling
Find Your Pickup Day
Trash and recycling are picked up once a week. Your day depends on your address. Look it up on the city's schedule tool — enter your address and it tells you your day and what week your recycling cart goes out.
🔍 Find my pickup day
Recycling — Every Other Week
Detroit uses a blue cart for recycling, picked up every other week on the same day as trash. Accepted: bottles, cans, cardboard, paper, and plastics #1–7. No plastic bags, no food waste, no Styrofoam.
♻️ What can I recycle?
Large Item / Bulk Pickup
Need to get rid of furniture, appliances, or mattresses? Detroit offers free large item pickup — schedule it online or call. Items must be at the curb by 6am on your scheduled day. Limit: 5 items per pickup.
📦 Schedule large item pickup
Yard Waste
Leaves, branches, and grass clippings go in paper bags or a separate bin — not your regular trash cart. Yard waste is collected seasonally (spring through fall). Branches must be bundled and under 4 feet.
🍂 Yard waste info
Missed Pickup or Illegal Dumping
If your trash wasn't picked up or someone dumped illegally near your home, report it through the Improve Detroit app or call Public Works. The city aims to respond within 2 business days.
📱 Report via Improve Detroit 📞 (313) 224-7000
🚗 Parking
Street Cleaning — No Parking Days
Most Detroit streets have designated street cleaning days posted on signs. Typically one side of the street is cleaned in the morning, the other in the afternoon. Check the signs on your block — they'll show day, time, and which months it applies.
🧹 Street maintenance info
Snow Emergency Parking
When a snow emergency is declared, you must move your car off major streets within 2 hours. Violations result in ticketing and towing. Sign up for snow emergency alerts by text or email so you're never caught off guard.
❄️ Snow emergency rules 🔔 Sign up for alerts
Pay Parking & Meters
Detroit uses pay-by-plate parking in metered areas downtown and Midtown. Pay at the kiosk or use the ParkDetroit app. Rates and time limits vary by zone. Most meters are enforced Mon–Sat 8am–6pm unless posted otherwise.
📱 ParkDetroit app 🅿️ Parking info
Residential Parking Permits
Some Detroit neighborhoods have residential permit zones — you'll need a permit sticker to park on your block during restricted hours. Apply through the city with proof of address. Annual fee applies.
🏠 Get a permit
Parking Tickets & Towing
Pay parking tickets online or in person at the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center. If your car was towed, call the Detroit Police Auto Pound at (313) 596-2500 to locate it. Fees increase the longer it sits.
💳 Pay a ticket 📞 Auto Pound
💧 Water & Utilities
Start or Transfer Water Service
New to Detroit? Set up your water account with Detroit Water & Sewerage Department (DWSD) within 10 days of moving in. You'll need your address, ID, and deposit. Do it online, by phone, or in person at 735 Randolph St.
💧 Start water service 📞 (313) 267-8000
Pay Your Water Bill
Pay online, by phone, by mail, or at authorized payment locations across Detroit. Bills are issued every two months. Set up autopay to avoid late fees. If you're struggling to pay, ask about the WRAP assistance program.
💳 Pay online 🤝 WRAP assistance
DTE Energy — Electric & Gas
Most Detroit residents get electricity and natural gas through DTE Energy. Start service online or by phone. If you're moving in, give DTE your move-in date in advance to avoid gaps. Assistance programs available for low-income households.
⚡ Start DTE service 📞 (800) 477-9000
Internet Options in Detroit
Major providers include Comcast Xfinity and AT&T. Eligible low-income households may qualify for the Affordable Connectivity Program — up to $30/month off your internet bill. Check availability by address.
📶 ACP discount program 💻 Detroit digital equity
🗳️ Voting & Civic Life
Register to Vote
Michigan allows same-day voter registration at the polls. Register online up to 15 days before an election, or in person at your local clerk's office or at the polls on Election Day with proof of residency.
✅ Register online 🗳️ Detroit Elections
Find Your Polling Place
Your polling place is assigned by address. Find yours on the Michigan Voter Information Center. Detroit also has early voting at multiple locations before Election Day — check the city clerk's site for dates.
📍 Find my polling place 📬 Absentee ballot
Find Your City Council Rep
Detroit has 7 district council members plus 2 at-large members. Your district rep is your direct contact for neighborhood issues, city services, and local concerns. Find yours by address on the council website.
🏛️ Find my council rep
Mayor's Office & City Services
The Mayor's Action Center connects residents to city departments, tracks service requests, and handles complaints. You can also use the Improve Detroit app to report issues and track resolution in real time.
🏙️ Mayor's Office 📱 Improve Detroit app
🏫 Schools & Education
Enroll in Detroit Public Schools
Detroit Public Schools Community District (DPSCD) is the main K–12 public school system. Enrollment is open year-round. You'll need proof of residency, immunization records, and prior school records. Call or visit the enrollment office.
📚 DPSCD enrollment 📞 (313) 873-6897
Find a School by Address
Use the DPSCD school finder to see which schools serve your address, including your assigned neighborhood school and schools of choice options. Detroit also has many public charter schools with open enrollment.
🗺️ School finder 🏫 Charter school info
Head Start & Early Childhood
Wayne County Head Start provides free early education for income-eligible children ages 0–5, including health, nutrition, and family support. Apply early — spots fill quickly. Great Start Readiness Program (GSRP) serves 4-year-olds.
👶 Head Start enrollment 🌟 GSRP Pre-K
Adult Education & GED
Detroit has multiple free adult education programs for residents who want to earn a GED, learn English, or upgrade job skills. Detroit at Work and community colleges offer free and low-cost options.
🎓 Adult education 🏛️ Wayne County Community College
🪪 ID, Licenses & Vehicle Registration
Michigan Driver's License or State ID
New Michigan residents must get a Michigan license within 30 days. Visit a Secretary of State branch — Detroit's closest is at 3046 W Grand Blvd. Bring your out-of-state license, proof of residency (two documents), and Social Security number.
🪪 Get a MI license 📍 Find SOS branch
Vehicle Registration
Register your vehicle within 30 days of establishing Michigan residency. You'll need your title, proof of insurance, and payment for registration tabs. Michigan requires no-fault auto insurance — make sure you're covered before you drive.
🚗 Register vehicle 💻 Do it online
Real ID
Starting May 2025, a Real ID-compliant driver's license or ID is required to board domestic flights and access federal buildings. When renewing or getting a new license, ask for Real ID — bring original documents (birth certificate, SSN card, two proofs of address).
✈️ Real ID info
Update Your Address
Moved within Michigan? Update your address with the Secretary of State within 10 days. You can do it online for free — no branch visit needed. Also update your voter registration, which you can do at the same time.
📬 Update address online
📋 Permits, Property & Taxes
Building Permits
Doing home improvements? Many projects require a building permit from BSEED — including additions, electrical work, plumbing, HVAC, decks, and fences. Unpermitted work can create legal and insurance problems. Check what's required before you start.
🔨 Get a permit 📞 BSEED (313) 224-2733
Property Taxes
Detroit property taxes are paid to the Wayne County Treasurer. Summer taxes are due July 31; winter taxes are due February 14. Homeowners may qualify for the Principal Residence Exemption (PRE) which reduces your taxable value — file with the Assessor's office.
🏠 Pay property taxes 📄 PRE exemption
Look Up Your Property Info
Use Detroit's property portal to look up your parcel number, assessed value, ownership history, zoning, and tax status. Useful when buying, selling, or disputing your assessment.
🔍 Property lookup 📊 Wayne County Assessor
Foreclosure Prevention
Falling behind on property taxes? Wayne County's Delinquent Tax Installment Plan (DTIP) lets you pay back taxes over time to prevent foreclosure. Act before March 31 of the third year of delinquency — after that, foreclosure proceeds.
🏡 Tax payment plans 🤝 UCHC housing help
🐾 Pets & Animals
Dog Licenses
All dogs in Detroit must be licensed annually through Wayne County. You'll need proof of rabies vaccination. Fees are lower for spayed/neutered dogs. Buy online or at the Wayne County Animal Shelter. Unlicensed dogs can result in fines.
🐕 License your dog
Animal Control & Stray Reporting
Report stray, injured, or dangerous animals to Detroit Animal Care & Control. They also handle cruelty complaints and wildlife issues. For loose dogs creating a safety threat, call the non-emergency police line.
🐾 Animal Control 📞 (313) 224-1426
Wayne County Animal Shelter
The Wayne County Animal Shelter takes in stray and surrendered animals. If you've lost a pet, check here first. They also run low-cost spay/neuter programs and adoption events. Located at 1 Brinker St, Detroit.
🏠 Wayne County shelter 📞 (313) 224-8333
🚨 Safety & Emergency
Emergency vs. Non-Emergency
911 is for life-threatening emergencies only. For non-emergency police matters — noise complaints, minor incidents, vandalism, or general concerns — call (313) 267-4600. Using 911 for non-emergencies slows response for real ones.
🆘 911 (emergencies) 📞 Non-emergency police
Know Your Police Precinct
Detroit has 12 police precincts. Knowing yours helps you connect with your local officers and attend community policing meetings. You can also report issues directly to your precinct's community relations officer.
🚔 Find my precinct
Emergency Alerts
Sign up for Detroit's emergency notification system to get text and email alerts about snow emergencies, boil water advisories, severe weather, and city closures. It takes under a minute to sign up.
🔔 Sign up for alerts
Fire Safety & Prevention
The Detroit Fire Department offers free smoke detector installation for Detroit residents. Call to request — they'll come to your home. Smoke detectors save lives; Detroit law requires working detectors on every floor.
🔥 Detroit Fire Dept 📞 (313) 224-4800
🌳 Neighborhood Life
Join or Start a Block Club
Block clubs are one of the best ways to meet neighbors, address local issues, and improve your block. Detroit has hundreds of active block clubs. Contact your City Council district office to find yours or get support starting one.
🏘️ Find your district
Detroit Parks & Recreation
Detroit has 300+ parks and a citywide recreation system with pools, community centers, and sports programs. Many programs are free or low-cost for Detroit residents. Find your nearest rec center and see programming online.
⛳ Parks & Recreation 🏊 Community centers
Detroit Public Library Card
A Detroit Public Library card is free for all Detroit residents. It unlocks 22 branches, free Wi-Fi, e-books, digital magazines, museum passes, tutoring, and job resources. Sign up in person at any branch with a photo ID and proof of address.
📚 Get a library card
Detroit Health Card
The Detroit Health Card is a free city program that gives Detroit residents discounts on groceries, health services, gym memberships, and local businesses. Apply online — you just need proof of Detroit residency.
💳 Get the Health Card
Report Issues & City Services
For general city service questions — potholes, streetlight outages, fallen trees, graffiti — use the Improve Detroit app. You can track your request status in the app and see what else has been reported on your block.
📱 Improve Detroit app
Free Tax Preparation
Detroit residents earning under $67,000 can get free tax prep through the Accounting Aid Society and other VITA sites. They also help you claim the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) — worth up to $7,000 for qualifying families.
💵 Free tax prep 💰 About EITC
